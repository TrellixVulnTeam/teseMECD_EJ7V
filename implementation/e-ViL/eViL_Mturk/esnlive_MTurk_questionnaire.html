Overview

Thank you for participating in this HIT and contributing to my PhD research! 🤗

The goal of this task is to assess the quality of explanations.

An explanation justifies an answer to a question.

This HIT contains 10 image-statement pairs. For each pair, you need to evaluate the quality of two given sentences that explain the relationship between the image and statement.

Task Description
  1. First, you will be shown an Image and a Statement about the image.
  2. You will then be asked which of the following relations best describe the image-statement pair: (i) contradiction, (ii) neutral, or (iii) entailment. Only one relation is correct and we know the correct answers. This is solely a check to make sure you understood the image-statement pair correctly. You will not be able to submit the HIT if too many answers are wrong. The three different answer options mean the following:
    • Entailment: There is enough evidence in the image to conclude that the statement is true.
    • Contradiction: There is enough evidence in the image to conclude that the statement is false.
    • Neutral: The evidence in the image is insufficient to draw a conclusion about the statement.
  3. You will then be shown two explanations that each try to justify this answer. The explanations are independent of each other and their order is meaningless.
  4. For each of the explanations, we ask two evaluation questions:
    • Given the image and the statement, does the explanation justify the answer?
    • If any, what are the shortcomings of the explanation?
Tips
  • Minor grammatical and style errors should be ignored (e.g. case sensitivity, missing periods, a missing pronoun etc.).
  • An explanation that just repeats or restates the statement is NOT a valid explanation.
  • A good approach to evaluating explanations is the following: Before looking at the explanations, think of an explanation yourself and then anchor your assessments based on that.

Quality checks and known answers are placed throughout the questionnaire!

EXAMPLE IMAGE:

Statement: The kids are not good at swimming.

1. What is the relationship between the image and the statement?
(There is enough evidence in the image to conclude that the statement is true)
(The evidence in the image is insufficient to draw a conclusion about the statement)
(There is enough evidence in the image to conclude that the statement is false)

For the next 2 explanations, answer the following:
Given the image and the statement, does the explanation justify the answer above?

Explanation #1: Wearing swimvests suggest that they are not good at swimming.
What are the shortcomings of Explanation #1?
This is a good explanation.
Explanation #2: Good swimmers can't be swimmers.
What are the shortcomings of Explanation #2?
The explanation doesn't justify the answer. Another shortcoming is that the explanation doesn't make grammatical sense.
Explanation #3: Because the water seems quite shallow.
What are the shortcomings of Explanation #3?
The justification isn't great. Good swimmers can also be in shallow water.
Explanation #4: Because the kids are wearing red swimvests.
What are the shortcomings of Explanation #4?
The explanation is good except for wrongly desribing the swimvests.
